About

Built for connection through different senses — without relying on your usual patterns.

What it is

A guided workshop that gradually trains safe blindfold dancing — then moves into a long silent block where the experience becomes deep, minimal, and focused.

connection pattern-breaking sensory awareness close embrace safety-first

Who it’s for

  • Zouk dancers from improver+ (below intermediate may find it challenging).
  • Trusted multi-dance participants may be invited case-by-case.
  • Solo registrations are welcome (rotation is part of the format).
  • 18+ only.

Format

Two parts. Training first. Then the silent blindfold block.

Part 1 — Preparation (≈3h)

  • Warm-up, grounding, and sensory orientation
  • Close-embrace safety & boundaries (shown and practiced)
  • Safety without sight: collisions, touch, exploration
  • Gradual accommodation to blindfolds

Part 2 — Silent Block (≈3h)

  • Blindfolded, no participant talking (safety exceptions exist)
  • Rotation + moments of anonymity
  • Exploration of new senses and connections
  • Closing debrief & grounding afterwards

Safety & Consent

Respected boundaries. Practiced signals. Clear rules. Safety overrides everything.
Consent baseline ›
  • No intimate touch, no surprises, no testing boundaries. Everything stays within socially appropriate close-embrace partner-dance boundaries.
  • No romantic escalation. No kissing, sexual comments, whispering, or “going beyond dancing”.
  • No-go zones are explicitly listed, shown, and repeated before the Silent Block.
Yellow / Red system (practiced) ›
  • Yellow: reduce intensity / slow down / increase space.
  • Red: full stop — freeze and give space immediately.
  • No explanation needed. Team actively monitor and can step in.
Touch boundaries (what’s allowed) ›

Close embrace is part of the format. Within defined limits (shown visually), the workshop may include:

  • Body waves (frame-based and open-position waves)
  • Chest-to-chest contact and kizomba-style hip contact within demonstrated boundaries

Not allowed: no-go zones, breathing in ears, kissing, or any exploratory hand placement outside instructed contact points.

Movement rules (blindfold part) ›
  • No lifts. No dips. No broad movements.
  • Head movement is minimal and strictly guided — no fast or large-range head motions.
  • Traveling is allowed only when safe for the exercise; The team may stop risky movement immediately.
The Silent Block rule (blindfolds) ›

Blindfolds stay on in the dance space at all times during the Silent Block.

If you need water, restroom, or support, raise your hand and a Team member will escort you to a private safe area (separate from the dance space). In the safe area, you may remove the blindfold if needed.

Returning to the Silent Block is usually possible, but may be declined if safety or container integrity requires it (e.g., medical concern, repeated distress, not following instructions, disruptive behavior, or safety risk to self/others).

If return is declined, you can rest in a chill area and may be allowed to join the final debrief. No refunds apply in this case.

Signals (silent block) ›
  • Stop with partner: trained hand squeeze → freeze + give space.
  • Call a team member: raise your hand (team members are trained to notice quickly).
  • Audible exception: if absolutely necessary, a short audible call is allowed to get help. Safety overrides silence.
Hygiene & sobriety ›
  • Please come sober. No alcohol or recreational drugs before or during the workshop.
  • Perfume discouraged (odorless antiperspirant is fine). Shower recommended.
  • Fresh shirt strongly recommended.
  • No rings/jewelry/watches; short nails recommended.
